The 2021 ICC Men's T20 World Cup was the seventh ICC Men's T20 World Cup tournament,[3][4] with the matches played in the United Arab Emirates and Oman from 17 October to 14 November 2021.[5][6] The West Indies were the defending champions,[7][8] but were eventually eliminated in the Super 12 stage.[9] Originally the event was scheduled to be held in Australia from 18 October to 15 November 2020,[10][11][12] but in July 2020, the International Cricket Council (ICC) confirmed that this tournament had been postponed, due to the COVID-19 pandemic.[13][14][15] In August 2020, the ICC confirmed that India would host the 2021 tournament as planned, with Australia being named as the host for the succeeding 2022 tournament.[16] However, in June 2021, the ICC announced that the tournament had been moved to the United Arab Emirates due to growing concerns over the COVID-19 pandemic situation in India, and a possible third wave of the pandemic in the country.[17] The tournament began on 17 October 2021,[5] with the tournament's final taking place on 14 November 2021.[18] The preliminary rounds of the tournament were played in the UAE and Oman.[19] New Zealand became the first team to reach the final, after they beat England by five wickets in the semi-finals.[20] It was the first time that New Zealand had qualified for a T20 World Cup final.[21] They were joined in the final by Australia, who beat Pakistan by five wickets in the second semi-final.[22] It was the second time that Australia had qualified for the final of the T20 World Cup, after reaching the final of the 2010 tournament.[23] In the final, Australia beat New Zealand by eight wickets to win their first T20 World Cup.[24] Mitchell Marsh was named the Player of the Match,[25] with David Warner being named the Player of the Tournament.[26]
